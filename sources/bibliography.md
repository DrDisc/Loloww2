## Primary Sources

### Official US Government Publications

**Morton, Louis.** *The Fall of the Philippines*. United States Army Center of Military History, CMH Pub 5-2, 1953.
- Official US Army history of the Philippine campaign
- Based on War Department records and after-action reports
- Definitive operational account

**US Department of State Office of the Historian.** "The Philippines, 1898-1946." *Milestones in the History of U.S. Foreign Relations*.
- https://history.state.gov/milestones/1899-1913/war
- Official diplomatic history
- Covers Spanish-American War through independence

**National Archives and Records Administration (NARA)**
- Philippine Scouts service records
- Unit rosters and organizational documents
- POW records and camp documentation
- Located: College Park, MD

**Veterans History Project, Library of Congress**
- https://www.loc.gov/vets/
- Oral histories and written accounts from WWII veterans
- Audio and video interviews

## Secondary Sources - Books

### Military History

**Whitman, John W.** *Bataan: Our Last Ditch*. New York: Hippocrene Books, 1990. ISBN 0870528777.
- Comprehensive tactical and operational analysis of Bataan campaign
- Includes Japanese sources and perspectives
- Quote on American artillery effectiveness

**Falk, Stanley L.** *Bataan: The March of Death*. New York: W.W. Norton, 1962.
- Detailed account of Bataan Death March
- Careful analysis of casualty figures
- Based on survivor testimony and official records

**Greenberger, Robert.** *The Bataan Death March: World War II Prisoners in the Pacific*. Compass Point Books, 2009. ISBN 978-0-7565-4095-1.
- Survivor testimonies
- Well-documented account of the march
- Accessible writing style

**Sides, Hampton.** *Ghost Soldiers: The Epic Account of World War II's Greatest Rescue Mission*. New York: Doubleday, 2001. ISBN 0-385-49565-9.
- Account of January 1945 Cabanatuan prison camp rescue
- Role of Filipino guerrillas
- Based on extensive interviews and research

**Toland, John.** *The Rising Sun: The Decline and Fall of the Japanese Empire, 1936-1945*. New York: Random House, 1970. ISBN 0-394-42968-5.
- Comprehensive Pacific War history
- Includes Japanese perspectives
- Pulitzer Prize winner

**Knox, Donald.** *Death March: The Survivors of Bataan*. New York: Harcourt Brace Jovanovich, 1981. ISBN 0-15-125065-1.
- Oral history compilation
- First-person accounts from survivors
- Covers Bataan, Death March, and POW camps

**Norman, Elizabeth M.** *We Band of Angels: The Untold Story of American Nurses Trapped on Bataan by the Japanese*. New York: Random House, 1999. ISBN 0-375-50291-4.
- Perspective on medical personnel during campaign
- Conditions in field hospitals
- POW experience of nurses

### Philippine History and Colonial Period

**Karnow, Stanley.** *In Our Image: America's Empire in the Philippines*. New York: Random House, 1989. ISBN 0-394-54975-0.
- Comprehensive history of American colonial period
- Cultural and political analysis
- Pulitzer Prize winner

**Linn, Brian McAllister.** *The Philippine War, 1899-1902*. Lawrence: University Press of Kansas, 2000. ISBN 0-7006-1225-3.
- Definitive account of Philippine-American War
- Based on extensive archival research
- Military and political analysis

**Steinberg, David Joel.** *The Philippines: A Singular and Plural Place*. 4th edition. Boulder: Westview Press, 2000. ISBN 0-8133-3755-1.
- Cultural and historical overview
- Context for American period
- Philippine perspective

**Constantino, Renato.** *A History of the Philippines: From the Spanish Colonization to the Second World War*. New York: Monthly Review Press, 1975. ISBN 0-85345-380-4.
- Filipino nationalist perspective
- Critical analysis of colonialism
- Social and economic history

### Recommended for Further Reading

**Agoncillo, Teodoro A.** *The Fateful Years: Japan's Adventure in the Philippines, 1941-1945*. Quezon City: R.P. Garcia Publishing, 1965.
- Philippine historian's perspective
- Comprehensive coverage of Japanese occupation
- Filipino resistance movements

**Jose, Ricardo T.** *The Philippine Army, 1935-1942*. Quezon City: Ateneo de Manila University Press, 1992.
- Detailed military organizational history
- Formation and training of Philippine Army vs. Philippine Scouts
- Pre-war preparations

**Connaughton, Richard.** *MacArthur and Defeat in the Philippines*. New York: Overlook Press, 2001. ISBN 1-58567-159-9.
- Critical analysis of MacArthur's command decisions
- Tactical and strategic mistakes
- British military historian's perspective

**Hastings, Max.** *Retribution: The Battle for Japan, 1944-45*. New York: Alfred A. Knopf, 2008. ISBN 978-0-307-26351-3.
- Broader Pacific War context
- Final year of war including Philippine liberation
- Multiple national perspectives

## Memoirs and Personal Accounts

### Published Memoirs

**Ramsey, Edwin Price, and Stephen J. Rivele.** *Lieutenant Ramsey's War: From Horse Soldier to Guerrilla Commander*. Washington, DC: Brassey's, 1990.
- First-person account from 26th Cavalry officer
- Led last US cavalry charge
- Guerrilla warfare in Central Luzon

**Dyess, William E.** *The Dyess Story: The Eye-Witness Account of the Death March from Bataan*. New York: G.P. Putnam's Sons, 1944.
- First published survivor account
- Escaped POW camp and reported atrocities
- Sparked American public outrage

**Gordon, Richard M., and Max Hastings.** *Horyo: Memoirs of an American POW*. St. Paul: Paragon House, 1999.
- Personal account of Bataan, Death March, POW camps
- Medical perspective (Gordon was medic)

**Hunt, Ray C., and Bernard Norling.** *Behind Japanese Lines: An American Guerrilla in the Philippines*. Lexington: University Press of Kentucky, 1986.
- American officer who led Filipino guerrillas
- Tactical accounts of resistance operations

## Citation Standards

This project uses **footnote citation format** following these guidelines:

### Books
Author Last Name, First Name. *Book Title*. Place: Publisher, Year.

### Articles
Author Last Name, First Name. "Article Title." *Journal Name* Volume, no. Issue (Year): Pages.

### Online Sources
"Article Title." *Website Name*. URL (Accessed Date).

Repository Name, Collection Name, Box/Folder Number, Document Description.

## Notes on Source Quality

### High Reliability
- Official US Army histories (Morton)
- Peer-reviewed academic books (Linn, Karnow)
- Contemporary official documents
- Cross-referenced Wikipedia articles with citations

### Medium Reliability
- Popular histories (Sides, Toland) - engaging but verify facts
- Memoirs - valuable but subjective, confirm with other sources
- Oral histories - important but memories can be imperfect

### Use With Caution
- Uncited online sources
- Social media posts
- Undocumented claims
- Politically motivated sources

### Verification Standard
For this project, all factual claims should ideally have:
1. Primary source documentation, OR
2. Agreement between 2+ reputable secondary sources, OR
3. Clear attribution to single source with caveat ("according to...")
