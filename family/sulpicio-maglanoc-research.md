## Philippine Division Background

### Unit History
- Established: 1921 as part of US Army Forces Far East (USAFFE)
- Composition: Two regiments of Philippine Scouts + support units
- Commander: Major General Jonathan Wainwright (later)
- Strength: Approximately 12,000 men at start of war

### Philippine Scouts Regiments
- 45th Infantry Regiment (PS)
- 57th Infantry Regiment (PS)
- 26th Cavalry Regiment (PS) - Famous for last US cavalry charge
- 24th Field Artillery Regiment (PS)
- 25th Field Artillery Regiment (PS)
- Various engineer, medical, and support units

### Enlistment Age
- Minimum age: Typically 18 (sometimes younger with parental consent)
- If Sulpicio was 19 in December 1941, he likely enlisted:
  - **Most likely:** 1940 (age 18)
  - **Possible:** 1941 (age 19)
  - **Less likely:** 1939 (age 17 - would need parental consent)

### Bataan Campaign
- Defended Bataan Peninsula: January-April 1942
- Fought despite starvation, disease, and lack of supplies
- Surrender: April 9, 1942
- Approximately 12,000 Americans and 66,000 Filipinos captured

## Bataan Death March

### Overview
- Date: April 9-18, 1942 (approximately)
- Route: Mariveles/Bagac to Camp O'Donnell (65-80 miles)
- Estimated deaths: 600-650 Americans, 5,000-18,000 Filipinos
- Conditions: Extreme heat, starvation, beatings, executions

### Sulpicio's Experience
- Age during march: 19 years old (turned 20 on May 2, 1942, during POW captivity)
- Young age may have helped survival (physical stamina)
