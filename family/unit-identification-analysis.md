## What We Know

### Confirmed Information
1. **Full Name**: Sulpicio P. Maglanoc (middle name unknown)
2. **Birth Date**: May 2, 1922
3. **Age at War Start**: 19 years old (December 8, 1941)
4. **Age at Bataan Surrender**: 19 years old (turned 20 on May 2, 1942, during captivity)
5. **Insignia**: Yellow/gold carabao (ox/water buffalo) head on red background
6. **Service**: Philippine Scout in US Army
7. **Campaign**: Defended Bataan, captured April 9, 1942
8. **Event**: Forced on Bataan Death March
9. **POW Status**: Survived POW camp(s)
10. **Post-war**: Died of lung cancer (date unknown)

### Key Deduction
The carabao insignia **definitively identifies** him as part of the **Philippine Division**, which was the only formation using this insignia.

### Critical New Information: His Age
At just **19 years old** when captured, Sulpicio was among the **youngest soldiers** on Bataan. This significantly impacts our analysis:
- **Enlistment**: Likely enlisted 1940-1941 (age 18-19)
- **Training**: Relatively new soldier (1-2 years service maximum)
- **Rank**: Almost certainly **Private** or **Private First Class**
- **Assignment**: Most likely basic infantry rifleman (not specialized role)

## Philippine Division Units on Bataan

### Infantry Regiments (Most Likely)

**1. 45th Infantry Regiment (Philippine Scouts)**
- **Strength**: Approximately 2,000 men
- **Commander**: Colonel Malcolm V. Fortier (American)
- **Composition**: 100% Filipino enlisted men, American officers, some Filipino officers
- **Combat Role**: 
  - Elite assault infantry
  - Held key defensive positions
  - Counterattacks during Battle of the Points
  - Used in critical sectors

**Key Actions:**
- January 6, 1942: SGT Jose Calugas (45th Infantry) won Medal of Honor
- January 22-February 13: Battle of the Points - eliminated Japanese landings
- February 3, 1942: 1LT Willibald Bianchi (45th Infantry) won Medal of Honor (posthumous)
- Held Orion-Bagac line until final collapse

**Probability for Sulpicio**: **HIGH** (40-45%)
- Largest Scout infantry regiment
- Saw extensive combat throughout campaign
- High casualty rate means many survivors on Death March

---

**2. 57th Infantry Regiment (Philippine Scouts)**
- **Strength**: Approximately 2,000 men
- **Commander**: Colonel George S. Clarke (American)
- **Composition**: 100% Filipino enlisted men, American officers, some Filipino officers
- **Combat Role**:
  - Bore brunt of initial Japanese attacks
  - Defended Abucay Line
  - Heavy fighting January 9-22, 1942

**Key Actions:**
- January 9-22, 1942: Defended Abucay Hacienda sector
- January 12, 1942: 2LT Alexander Nininger (57th Infantry) won Medal of Honor (posthumous)
- Sustained heavy casualties early in campaign
- Reinforced and continued fighting

**Probability for Sulpicio**: **HIGH** (40-45%)
- Equal size to 45th Infantry
- Extensive combat service
- Many survivors became POWs

---

**3. 31st Infantry Regiment (United States)**
- **NOT a Philippine Scout unit** - All American
- Sulpicio would NOT have been in this unit (he was Filipino)

### Cavalry Regiment (Possible)

**4. 26th Cavalry Regiment (Philippine Scouts)**
- **Strength**: Approximately 800 men (smaller than infantry regiments)
- **Commander**: Colonel Clinton A. Pierce (American)
- **Composition**: Filipino enlisted men, American officers
- **Role**:
  - Horse-mounted cavalry (last in US Army)
  - Reconnaissance and screening
  - Famous last cavalry charge (Lieutenant Edwin Ramsey)
  - Eventually dismounted due to horse casualties

**Key Actions:**
- December 1941: Last US cavalry charge at Morong
- Conducted delaying actions and reconnaissance
- Later fought dismounted as infantry
- Many horses killed; soldiers fought on foot

**Probability for Sulpicio**: **LOW-MODERATE** (10-15%)
- Smaller unit = fewer personnel
- Elite unit requiring cavalry training and horsemanship
- At age 18-19, unlikely to have specialized cavalry training
- Given his young age, basic infantry more probable

**5. 24th Field Artillery Regiment (Philippine Scouts)**

## How to Narrow It Down

### Questions to Investigate

**About His Role:**
1. Did he ever mention what weapon he used?
   - Rifle = Infantry
   - Machine gun = Infantry (weapons company)
   - Horses = Cavalry
   - Artillery pieces = Artillery

2. Did he describe his daily duties before the war?
   - Patrol/reconnaissance = Cavalry
   - Marching, guard duty = Infantry
   - Gun drills, calculations = Artillery

3. Did he mention his unit number or commander's name?
   - "45th" or "57th" = Infantry
   - "26th" = Cavalry
   - "24th" or "25th" = Artillery

4. Where was he when war started?
   - Fort McKinley = Infantry
   - Fort Stotsenburg = Cavalry
   - Various locations = Artillery

5. Did he describe specific battles?
   - Abucay Hacienda = Likely 57th Infantry
