# Lolo's War: The Philippine Scouts and the Pacific Theater

A comprehensive historical research project documenting the role of Filipino soldiers in World War II, with a focus on the Philippine Scouts, the Bataan Death March, and the Pacific Theater campaigns.

**Dedicated to Sulpicio P. Maglanoc** (May 2, 1922 - ?) - Philippine Scout, Bataan Death March survivor, and grandfather whose stories inspired this work.

---

## Project Overview

This repository contains research, sources, and content for a long-form historical article examining the Filipino contribution to World War II in the Pacific Theater. The project combines rigorous historical research from Philippine, American, and Japanese sources with veteran testimonials and battle tactics analysis.

### Target Audience
- Filipino communities seeking to understand their WWII heritage
- Military history enthusiasts interested in Pacific Theater operations
- Family members and descendants of WWII veterans
- Educators and researchers

### Content Strategy
- **Primary**: Single long-form article with academic rigor and historical accuracy
- **Secondary**: Video shorts campaign featuring key moments, testimonials, and tactical breakdowns

## Philippine Scouts Background

### Unit Identification
**Insignia**: Yellow/Gold Carabao (water buffalo) head on red shield background
- This was the **Philippine Division** insignia
- Philippine Scouts were elite US Army units composed of Filipino soldiers
- Commanded by American officers with Filipino enlisted men and NCOs

### Historical Significance
- Established 1901 as part of US colonial military structure
- Considered among the best jungle fighters in the US Army
- Fought with distinction in Bataan despite being outnumbered and undersupplied
- Many became POWs during Bataan Death March
- Survivors formed core of guerrilla resistance

## Sulpicio P. Maglanoc - Research Notes

### Known Information
- **Full Name**: Sulpicio P. Maglanoc (middle name unknown)
- **Birth Date**: May 2, 1922
- **Age at War**: 19 years old (one of the youngest soldiers on Bataan)
- **Service**: Philippine Scout, US Army
- **Unit**: Philippine Division (Carabao insignia) - likely 45th or 57th Infantry Regiment
- **Likely Rank**: Private or Private First Class (young recruit)
- **Major Event**: Captured April 9, 1942 and forced on Bataan Death March
- **Post-War**: POW camp survivor; died of lung cancer
- **Testimony**: Shared stories of POW camp horrors
