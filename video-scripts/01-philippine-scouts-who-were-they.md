# Video Short Script: Philippine Scouts - Who Were They?

**Duration:** 60-90 seconds  
**Platform:** YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Instagram Reels

## Script

### Visual: Historic photo of Philippine Scouts in uniform with carabao insignia

**Narrator (VO):**
"This is the carabao - the water buffalo of the Philippines. But it's also the symbol of some of America's most elite fighting men in World War II."

### Visual: Close-up of Philippine Division shoulder patch

**Narrator (VO):**
"The Philippine Scouts were units of the United States Army, composed entirely of Filipino soldiers. Formed in 1901, they became known as the best jungle fighters in the entire US military."

### Visual: Footage/photos of Scouts training, marching

**Narrator (VO):**
"By 1941, nearly 12,000 Philippine Scouts served in the Philippine Division alongside American troops. They were professional soldiers - elite warriors with better training and equipment than most Philippine Army units."

### Visual: Photo of Filipino and American officers together

**Narrator (VO):**
"Commanded by American officers but increasingly led by Filipinos, including graduates of West Point, the Scouts represented a unique military tradition."

### Visual: Map of Philippines with carabao insignia

**Narrator (VO):**
"When Japan invaded the Philippines in December 1941, the Philippine Scouts would face their greatest test - defending Bataan against overwhelming odds."

### Visual: Text overlay with key facts

**Text on Screen:**
- Formed: 1901
- Strength in 1941: 11,972 soldiers
- Elite US Army units
- Filipino soldiers, American command

**Narrator (VO):**
"Their story is one of courage, sacrifice, and a complex colonial relationship that defined Filipino service in WWII."

### Closing Visual: Carabao insignia with text "Lolo's War"

**End Card:**
"Follow for more stories of Filipino heroes in WWII"
