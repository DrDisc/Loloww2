# Resource Guide: Where to Find Additional Tactical Maps and Photos

**Goal**: Locate high-quality, historically accurate maps and photos to complete the visual documentation of the Philippine Scouts and Bataan Campaign.

---

## **Priority Maps Needed**

### **1. Philippine Division Sector Maps (CRITICAL)**
**What you need**: Detailed maps showing exactly where the 45th and 57th Infantry (Philippine Scouts) were positioned on the Orion-Bagac Line.

#### **Where to find**:

**A. US Army Center of Military History**
- Website: https://history.army.mil/
- Navigate to: `Publications > CMH Pub 72-2, The Fall of the Philippines`
- Direct link: https://history.army.mil/books/wwii/fall-of-ph/index.htm
- Look for: Maps in Chapters 14-20 (The Battle of Bataan)
- **Specific maps to find**:
  - Map 17: The Orion-Bagac Line (you have this)
  - Map 18: II Corps Sector Detail (NEED THIS - shows 45th/57th PS)
  - Map 19: I Corps Sector
  - Map 20: Japanese Attack Plan, 3-9 April

**B. US National Archives (NARA)**
- Records Group 77: Records of the Office of the Chief of Engineers
  - Series: World War II Theater Maps
  - Box numbers to request: RG 77, Entry 363, Boxes 1-15 (Philippines)
- Records Group 407: Records of the Adjutant General's Office
  - Entry 427: World War II Operations Reports
  - Look for: "Philippine Division After Action Reports, 1942"
  
**How to access**:
- Online catalog: https://catalog.archives.gov/
- Search terms: "Bataan maps 1942", "Philippine Division tactical", "Orion Bagac"
- Many maps digitized - can download high-res
- For non-digitized: submit request via NARA's online ordering system

**C. MacArthur Memorial Archives**
- Location: Norfolk, VA (physical visit) or online database
- Website: https://www.macarthurmemorial.org/
- Collection: RG-10 (Military Records of Douglas MacArthur)
- Look for: USAFFE (United States Army Forces in the Far East) operational maps
- Email research requests: library@macarthurmemorial.org

### **2. Bataan Death March Detailed Maps**

**What you need**: 
- Route with POW assembly points
- Stopping points (San Fernando, Capas)
- Camp O'Donnell layout
- Survivor testimonial locations

#### **Where to find**:

**A. American Defenders of Bataan and Corregidor Memorial Society (ADBC)**
- Website: https://www.adbc-descendants.org/
- They maintain detailed Death March documentation
- Contact for research access - very helpful to family members
- May have maps created by survivors

**B. Battling Bastards of Bataan**
- Website: http://battlingbastardsbataan.com/
- Survivor testimonials often reference specific locations
- Map resources section

**C. Library of Congress - Veterans History Project**
- Website: https://www.loc.gov/vets/
- Search: "Bataan Death March maps"
- Filter by: Photographs, Maps, Documents
- Many survivors donated hand-drawn maps

**D. Published Sources** (check maps inside):
- *Bataan: The March of Death* by Stanley L. Falk
- *Tears in the Darkness* by Michael Norman (has detailed route map)
- *Ghost Soldiers* by Hampton Sides (Camp O'Donnell layout)

### **3. Philippine Scouts Unit Photos**

**What you need**:
- 45th Infantry Regiment (PS) in action
- 57th Infantry Regiment (PS) in action
- Philippine Division training at Fort McKinley (pre-war)
- Philippine Division insignia (carabao) in use (on uniforms, flags)

#### **Where to find**:

**A. US Army Heritage and Education Center (USAHEC)**
- Location: Carlisle, PA
- Online catalog: https://ahec.armywarmemorial.org/
- Collections to search:
  - Philippine Scouts Photo Collection
  - WWII Pacific Theater Photo Archives
- Search terms: "Philippine Scouts", "45th Infantry", "57th Infantry", "Fort McKinley"
- Digital requests: usarmy.carlisle.awc.mbx.ahec-reference@army.mil

**B. National WWII Museum**
- Website: https://www.nationalww2museum.org/
- Digital collections: https://www.ww2online.org/
- Search: "Philippine Scouts", "Bataan"
- Extensive Pacific Theater photo collection

**C. Filipino American National Historical Society (FANHS)**
- Website: https://fanhs-national.org/
- Specifically focused on Filipino American military history
- Photo archives of Philippine Scouts
- Contact for research: info@fanhs-national.org

**D. University of the Philippines Diliman - Filipiniana Division**
- Pre-war and wartime photo collections
- Contact via: https://www.mainlib.upd.edu.ph/
- May require in-person visit or hire researcher in Manila

**E. National Historical Commission of the Philippines (NHCP)**
- Website: https://nhcp.gov.ph/
- Photo archives of Filipino soldiers in WWII
- Email: info@nhcp.gov.ph

### **4. Japanese Maps and Photos (Opposition Perspective)**

**What you need**:
- Japanese operational maps of Bataan assault
- Photos from Japanese side (context, not glorification)
- Japanese unit dispositions

#### **Where to find**:

**A. National Institute for Defense Studies (Japan)**
- Website: http://www.nids.mod.go.jp/ (Japanese and English)
- War History Office Archives
- Captured/translated Japanese maps at US NARA
- Search NARA for: "Japanese Monographs" - post-war reports written by Japanese officers

**B. US National Archives - Captured Japanese Records**
- Records Group 242: National Archives Collection of Foreign Records Seized
- Entry: Japanese Army and Navy Archives
- Search: "14th Army Philippines 1942"
- Many digitized, searchable online

**C. Australian War Memorial**
- Website: https://www.awm.gov.au/
- Extensive Pacific War collection including Japanese materials
- Digital collection search: "Philippines 1942"

## **Step-by-Step: How to Request from NARA**

### **Finding Maps Online**
1. Go to: https://catalog.archives.gov/
2. Search box: `"Philippine Division" maps 1942`
3. Filters: 
   - Format: Maps and Charts
   - Date: 1941-1942
4. Look for digitized copies (camera icon)
5. Download high-resolution versions

### **Requesting Non-Digitized Materials**
1. Create account at NARA
2. Identify Record Group and Box number from catalog
3. Submit online request: https://catalog.archives.gov/research/order/online
4. Wait 6-8 weeks for scan
5. Cost: Usually free for limited pages, ~$0.80/page for large orders

### **Visiting NARA in Person** (College Park, MD)
- Best for bulk research
- Can photograph/scan materials yourself
- Research room hours: Mon-Fri 9am-5pm
- Free researcher card required

## **Citation and Attribution**

When you find maps/photos:
- Always note the source
- Check copyright/usage (most pre-1978 US Gov = public domain)
- Attribute in your project: "Source: US Army Center of Military History"
- Keep metadata: photographer, date, location, units depicted

**Template**:
```
Filename: orion-bagac-line-jan-1942.jpg
Source: US Army Center of Military History, CMH Pub 72-2, Map 17
Date: January 27, 1942
Description: Main defensive line across Bataan Peninsula
Units: Philippine Division (45th, 57th Infantry PS), I and II Corps
Copyright: Public Domain (US Government work)
```
