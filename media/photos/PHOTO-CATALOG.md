### **Surrender and Capture**

#### `Surrender_on_Bataan.jpg`
- **Date**: April 9, 1942
- **Location**: Bataan Peninsula
- **Description**: American and Filipino troops surrendering to Japanese forces
- **Significance**: **This is the moment Sulpicio Maglanoc became a POW**
- **Key detail**: Largest US surrender in history (76,000 troops)
- **Source**: US Army / Public Domain
- **Emotional weight**: HIGH - this is your lolo's worst day

#### `Surrender_of_American_troops_at_Corregidor.jpg`
- **Date**: May 6, 1942
- **Location**: Corregidor Island
- **Description**: US troops surrendering at Corregidor (1 month after Bataan)
- **Significance**: Final US stronghold falls; MacArthur had already evacuated to Australia
- **Source**: US Army Signal Corps / Public Domain
- **Note**: Bataan surrendered April 9; Corregidor held out until May 6

### **Bataan Death March** ‚ö†Ô∏è

#### `1280px-Along_the_March_of_Death.jpg`
- **Date**: April 10-24, 1942
- **Location**: Bataan to Camp O'Donnell
- **Description**: American and Filipino POWs marching under Japanese guard
- **Significance**: **Sulpicio Maglanoc was in this march** - 65 miles in extreme heat
- **Casualties**: Estimated 5,000-18,000 died from execution, exhaustion, disease
- **Source**: US Army Signal Corps (taken by Japanese, captured post-war) / Public Domain
- **Emotional weight**: EXTREME - this shows your lolo's suffering
- **Usage note**: Handle with appropriate historical context and respect

#### `March_of_Death_-_Resting_02.jpg`
- **Date**: April 1942
- **Location**: Along Death March route
- **Description**: POWs resting during the march, clearly exhausted and malnourished
- **Significance**: Shows the physical condition of survivors like Sulpicio
- **Source**: Japanese photographer (captured post-war) / Public Domain
- **Key detail**: Many who sat down like this were bayoneted for "refusing to march"
- **Usage note**: Powerful testimony to survival; use respectfully

#### `bataan-death-march-route.svg`
- **Format**: SVG vector graphic
- **Description**: Map showing Death March route from Mariveles to Camp O'Donnell
- **Key stops**: Mariveles ‚Üí Balanga ‚Üí Orani ‚Üí Lubao ‚Üí San Fernando (rail) ‚Üí Capas ‚Üí Camp O'Donnell
- **Distance**: ~65 miles on foot, ~25 miles by rail in overcrowded boxcars
- **Duration**: 5-10 days depending on group
- **Usage**: Essential reference for understanding the march route

## **Philippine Scouts Training & Pre-War** (`/media/photos/philippine-scouts/`)

#### `Philippine_Scouts_1905.jpg`
- **Date**: 1905
- **Location**: Philippines (likely Manila area)
- **Description**: Early Philippine Scouts unit formation
- **Significance**: Shows origins of the organization; pre-dates Sulpicio's service by ~35 years
- **Uniform**: US Army khaki with campaign hats
- **Research value**: Historical continuity of Philippine Scouts tradition
- **Use for**: Background section on Scout history (1901-1946)

#### `philippine-scouts-training-fort-mckinley.jpg`
- **Date**: 1930s-1940s (pre-war)
- **Location**: Fort McKinley (now Fort Bonifacio), Manila
- **Description**: Philippine Scouts training exercises
- **Significance**: Where Sulpicio likely trained before war
- **Fort McKinley**: Main Philippine Scouts base; elite training facility
- **Use for**: Showing pre-war professionalism and readiness

#### `Instructing_Filipino_cadets.jpg`
- **Date**: Pre-war (likely 1930s-1941)
- **Location**: Philippines
- **Description**: US officers instructing Filipino cadets
- **Significance**: Shows US-Filipino military collaboration structure
- **Command structure**: American officers, Filipino enlisted men and NCOs (typical of Philippine Scouts)
- **Use for**: Explaining colonial military relationship

#### `Marines_teach_Filipino_cadets.jpg`
- **Date**: Pre-war
- **Location**: Philippines (possibly Cavite or Olongapo)
- **Description**: US Marines training Filipino cadets
- **Note**: May be Philippine Constabulary or Philippine Army, not necessarily Scouts
- **Use for**: Training and preparation context

#### `4th_Marines_at_the_philippines_in_January_1942.jpg`
- **Date**: January 1942
- **Location**: Philippines (likely Corregidor)
- **Unit**: 4th Marine Regiment
- **Description**: US Marines during early defense of Philippines
- **Significance**: 4th Marines fought alongside Philippine Scouts on Bataan and Corregidor
- **Timeline**: January 1942 = after Japanese invasion, during defensive phase
- **Use for**: Allied forces context; multi-service defense

## **Photo Usage Guidelines**

### **For Article/Video**
- **Atrocity photos**: Use sparingly, with historical context and content warnings
- **Surrender photos**: Frame as "honorable resistance to the end, not shameful defeat"
- **Death March photos**: Essential for truth-telling, but handle with gravity
- **Victory/heroism photos**: Balance the narrative; not all tragedy
